Ingredients

  • Dough
  • Potatoes (peeled and boiled until soft)
  • Green chilies (finely chopped, adjust to taste)
  • Fresh cilantro (coriander leaves, finely chopped)
  • Ginger (grated or finely minced)
  • Garam masala (optional)
  • Cumin seeds (whole or crushed)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Red chili powder (optional, for added heat)
  • Amchur (dry mango powder) or lemon juice (for tanginess)
  • Oil or ghee (for cooking the parathas)

Steps

  1. Prepare the dough: Combine whole wheat flour, salt, and a little oil or ghee in a mixing bowl. Gradually add water and knead the dough until smooth and soft. Cover and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Make the potato filling: Mash boiled potatoes and mix with green chilies, cilantro, ginger, spices, salt, and lemon juice or amchur. Optionally, add chopped onions, garlic paste, and other spices for extra flavor.
  3. Divide the dough and filling: Divide the rested dough into equal-sized balls. Roll out a dough ball, place some potato filling in the center, and seal the edges to form a stuffed ball.
  4. Shape the parathas: Flatten the stuffed ball and roll it out into a round paratha.
  5. Cook the parathas: Cook the rolled-out paratha on a hot griddle or skillet until golden brown spots appear on both sides. Drizzle oil or ghee around the edges while cooking.
  6. Serve hot: Remove the cooked parathas from the griddle and serve hot with yogurt, pickle, or chutney.
